POSYDON v2
SUMMER SCHOOL 2025

22-26 September, SkAI
John Hancock Center, Chicago, IL

The first POSYDON school

Learn how to use POSYDON, a state-of-the-art binary population synthesis code now in its second version, and discover its unique capabilities. This week-long workshop aims to familiarize participants with the code so they can effectively incorporate it into their own research.


Through a series of hands-on labs, we will explore exciting astrophysical scenarios such as mass-transfer stability, supernovae, binary black-hole populations, and more, showcasing how POSYDON can advance your research goals. By the end of the school, you will be ready to apply your newfound expertise, share your knowledge with colleagues, and push single and binary stellar population research to new frontiers.

Registration Open

Everyone interested should apply, but please have in mind:

  • We request that all participants plan to attend the entire week of the school (Sept. 22 - 26th).
  • Applicants are not expected to know fully how to use POSYDON, but are expected to have it installed prior to arriving (installation instructions here).
  • Familiarity with Python and command line usage is expected.
  • Undergraduate (BSc), master (MSc), and graduate (PhD) students, please obtain the permission of your advisor before applying. We will contact your advisor to confirm that you meet the availability and preparedness requirements.

School Venue

The school is hosted jointly by the SkAI institute and Northwestern University, who will provide the computational facilities required by the classes.

John Hancock Center, Chicago, IL

Our first POSYDON school is located inside of the John Hancock Center (875 N, Michigan Ave.), located in the Magnificent Mile district of Chicago, Illinois and an iconic member of the city's skyline.
